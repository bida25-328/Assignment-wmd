RARE SNAKES

Venomous

King Cobra

The world's longest venomous snake, native to South and Southeast Asia. A resident of our Park 1 enclosure with controlled viewing available daily.

Black Mamba

Africa's fastest snake and one of the most feared. Our specimen is housed in a state-of-the-art biosecure enclosure at Park 2.

Puff Adder

Responsible for the most snakebite fatalities in Africa due to its wide range and cryptic camouflage. A critical focus of our anti-venom research.

Non-Venomous

Green Tree Python

A strikingly beautiful arboreal constrictor from New Guinea. Famous for coiling over branches in a symmetrical resting posture. Safe and popular with visitors.

Emerald Tree Boa

Often confused with the Green Tree Python, this South American species has a unique heat-sensing ability. A highlight of our guided night tours.

African Rock Python

Africa's largest snake — our specimen stretches over 5 metres. Despite its size it is non-venomous, using constriction to subdue prey.

Our Story

Mission

Our mission is to educate the public about the vital role snakes play in our ecosystems. Through conservation, research, and hands-on experiences, we aim to change the way people perceive these remarkable reptiles — from feared creatures to celebrated neighbours in our natural world.


We work with wildlife authorities, schools, and international partners to ensure that endangered snake species receive the protection they deserve. Every visit supports our breeding and rehabilitation programmes.

Origin

Serpentine was founded in 2008 by herpetologist Dr. Amara Dube, who spent two decades studying snake populations across sub-Saharan Africa. What began as a small rescue operation from her farm has grown into a nationally recognised sanctuary housing over 80 species.


Today, Serpentine operates two parks and partners with universities across the region to advance snake research and anti-venom development.
